MOSQ DETECTO

TABUC SUBA, JARO DISTRICT

DESCRIPTION:

The Place:
Barangay Tabuc Suba is a large residential and commercial area located along major thoroughfares in Jaro, Iloilo City. It is bounded by the Jaro River and contains multiple low-lying areas. Because of the quick pace of subdivision developments and channels where rainwater can pool in clogged drainage ditches, it is an ideal environment for mosquitoes to breed if local residents aren't careful.

Dengue Situation:
According to community surveillance data from the Iloilo City Health Office (CHO), Tabuc Suba is frequently marked as a critical area for dengue tracking during rainy cycles due to high population density. The neighborhood encounters periodic case clustering, meaning small groups of households in close proximity report infections around the same time. Consequently, health workers closely monitor localized transmission to eliminate breeding spots.

Health Efforts & Actions:
• Local Inspections: Barangay Health Workers regularly carry out house-to-house 4S campaigns to help families locate and drain stagnant water in storage drums, flower pots, and plastic containers.
• Targeted Spraying: When active case clusters are detected, the city sanitation office deploys emergency misting or thermal fogging to suppress the adult mosquito population.
• Early Checkups: Community members are strongly advised to visit the Jaro health center immediately for diagnostic blood testing if they exhibit sudden high fevers, allowing for prompt medical care.
