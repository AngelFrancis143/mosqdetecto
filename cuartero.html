MOSQ DETECTO

CUARTERO, JARO DISTRICT

DESCRIPTION:

The Place:
Barangay Cuartero is a busy, tightly packed neighborhood in Jaro, which is the largest and most populated district in Iloilo City. Due to the closely packed houses that are close together and rainwater can easily gather in yards and gutters, it is a prime spot for mosquitoes to lay eggs if residents aren't careful.

Dengue Situation:
While dengue cases across Iloilo City have generally been going down, Barangay Cuartero is still a high-priority area. The City Health Office has flagged it for "case clustering," which means several people in the same neighborhood blocks have caught dengue around the same time. Because of this, health teams are actively searching the area to find and destroy hidden mosquito breeding sites.

Health Efforts & Actions:
• Local Inspections: Barangay Health Workers go door-to-door to help families check for and dump out standing water in containers, pots, and drums.
• Targeted Spraying: When a cluster of cases is found, the city conducts localized misting (spraying) to get rid of adult mosquitoes.
• Early Checkups: Residents are told to visit the nearest health center immediately if they develop a sudden high fever so they can be tested and treated early.
