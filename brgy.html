Barangay's

The province and city of Iloilo are made up of hundreds of vibrant barangays, ranging from busy urban districts to quiet coastal and farming communities. Because people live so close together and many areas sit right along rivers or floodways, these neighborhoods have really tight-knit communities. However, the tropical climate and tricky drainage systems also mean they face some tough public health challenges, especially when the rainy season hits.

Dengue is one of the biggest issues here, with cases regularly spiking in specific hotspots. Barangay Tabuc Suba in Jaro is usually hit hard because it's densely populated and right by the water, making it easy for mosquitoes to breed. Other neighborhoods like Cuartero and Balabago also frequently see rising case numbers due to stagnant rainwater collecting in residential areas. Whenever these spikes happen, local health officials usually have to step in quickly, pushing for community cleanups to clear out breeding sites before things get out of hand.

Our immediate goal is to focus on Cuartero and Tabuc Suba since they are near our school, with Balabago planned for future development.
